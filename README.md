Data Analytics
Product Category Distribution (Bar Chart):
Insight:
   1.Clothing and Electronics categories show the highest number of transactions.
   2.Beauty have fewer transactions, which indicatees selective purchases.
Conclusion:
   1.Customers purchase clothing and beauty products more frequently,
   suggesting these categories drive regular customer engagement and repeat sales.

Sales Trend Over Time (Line Chart):
Insight:
   1.Sales fluctuate over time with noticeable peaks and drops.
   2.Certain periods show increased sales activity, indicating seasonality.
Conclusion:
   1.The presence of seasonal trends suggests that targeted promotions and 
    inventory planning during peak periods can significantly improve revenue performance.

Revenue by Product Category (Bar Chart):
Insight:
    1.Clothing contributes the highest total revenue.
    2.Electronics generate high-value transactions but at lower frequency.
Conclusion:
    1.While clothing dominates revenue through volume sales, electronics contribute through high-value purchases, 
     indicating a balanced product strategy which is beneficial.

Quantity Distribution(Histogram):
Insight:
    1.Most customers purchase 1 to 3 items per transaction.
    2.Bulk purchases are relatively rare and they purchase 3 to 4 of items.
Conclusion:
   1.Customer buying behavior favors small-quantity purchases, suggesting that bundle offers or 
   discounts could encourage higher transaction volumes.

Spending Distribution by Category (Box Plot):
Insight:
   1.Electronics show higher median spending and wider variation.
   2.Clothing and Beauty have more consistent spending patterns.
Conclusion:
   1.Electronics purchases involve higher spending and variability, while clothing and beauty products provide stable and predictable revenue streams.
